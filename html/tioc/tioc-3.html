Title of the Experiment : ANALYSIS OF AMMONIA CONTENT

Practical Significance:

Concentration of compound is the basic requirement in chemical industry. Such analysis is performed by using titration method. While performing such practical, accuracy is very important.

Relevant Program Outcomes (POs):

PO 1: Basic and Discipline specific knowledge: Apply knowledge of basic mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals and engineering specialization to solve the chemical engineering problems.

PO 4: Engineering Tools, Experimentation and Testing: Apply modern engineering tools and appropriate technique to conduct standard tests and measurements.

Practical Outcome

Determine the ammonia content of the given sample of fertilizer.

Theory

It is the measure of Percentage purity or concentration of the compound in the given sample by using titration method.

The reaction's equivalence point is the point at which the titrant has exactly neutralized the acid or base in the unknown analyte; if you know the volume and concentration of the titrant at the equivalence point, you can calculate the concentration of a base or acid in the unknown solution.

Experimental Set up:



Reagents -

1. Standard sodium hydroxide (0.1N)
2. Standard Hydrochloric acid (0.1N)
3. Phenolphthalein indicator

Apparatus -

1. Hydrometer
2. Conical flasks
3. Volumetric flask
4. Pipette
5. Burette
6. Thermometer
7. Cylindrical Vessel

Chemical Reaction -

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O


NH4NO3+NH3 → NaNO3+ H2O


Procedure -

  1. Wash the glassware with water and rinse with respective solution to be handled.
  2. Weigh accurately 0.5gm of fertilizer sample.
  3. Dissolve it in a 100ml of 0.1N NaOH solution in a conical flask.
  4. Keep funnel on the flask to avoid evaporation losses.
  5. Heat the reaction mixture so that ammonium nitrate hydrolyses with evolution of the ammonia gas.
  6. Confirm total removal of ammonia by change in the colour of yellow paper
  7. Carry out blank titration between 0.1N HCl solution and 0.1N NaOH solution.
  8. Cool the reaction mixture initially prepared and titrate it against 0.1N HCl solution.
  9. Perform the titration until a constant burette reading is obtained.
