Title of the Experiment : ANALYSIS OF SODA ASH

Practical Significance:

Concentration of compound is the basic requirement in chemical industry. Such analysis is performed by using titration method. While performing such practical, accuracy is very important.

Relevant Program Outcomes (POs):

PO 1: Basic and Discipline specific knowledge: Apply knowledge of basic mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals and engineering specialization to solve the chemical engineering problems.

PO 4: Engineering Tools, Experimentation and Testing: Apply modern engineering tools and appropriate technique to conduct standard tests and measurements.

Practical Outcome

To analyse the sample of soda ash to determine the amount of (A) sodium hydroxide (B)sodium carbonate and (C) sodium sulphate present in it.

Theory

A known volume of soda ash solution is titrated first with standard hydrochloric acid using phenolphthalein indicator wherein the end point is the point at which all the sodium hydroxide is converted to sodium chloride and all the sodium carbonate is converted to sodium bicarbonate. Next, the solution is further titrated with standard hydrochloric acid after adding methyl orange indicator wherein the end point is the point at which all the sodium bicarbonate is converted to sodium chloride.

The reactions taken place are:

Step 1 : NaOH + HCl ü°™ NaCl + H2O
Na2CO3 + HCl ü°™ NaHCO3 + NaCl

Step 2 : NaHCO3 + HCl ü°™ NaCl + H2O + CO2

We observe that sodium carbonate is neutralized in two steps and equal amount of hydrochloric acid is consumed in both the steps.

Volume of hydrochloric acid to neutralize sodium carbonate = 2 (y-x)

Volume of hydrochloric acid to neutralize= [ y-2 (y-x)] sodium hydroxide.

Experimental Set up:



Reagents -

1. Standard hydrochloric acid (0.1N)
2. Barium chloride solution
3. Silver nitrate solution
4. Phenolphthalein indicator
5. Methyl orange indicator.

Apparatus -

1. Volumetric flask 250ml
2. Pipette
3. Burette
4. Conical flasks 250 ml
5. Beaker
6. Desiccators
7. Bunsen Burner
8. funnel
9. Crucible
10. Ashless filter paper

Procedure -

  1. Pipette out 25 ml. of the given soda ash solution into a 250 ml. volumetric flask.
  2. Make up the solution to 250 ml. by adding water free from carbon dioxide.
  3. Pipette out 10 ml. of this made-up solution into a 250 ml. conical flask.
  4. Add 8-10 drops of phenolphthalein indicator and titrate the contents of the conical flask with standard hydrochloric acid until colour disappears after addition of a single drop.
  5. Note down the burette reading. Then add 1-2 drops of methyl orange indicator to the titrate solution.
  6. The solution turns yellow continue the titration until the solution becomes a permanent pink and take the burette reading again. Perform the experiment until constant readings are obtained
