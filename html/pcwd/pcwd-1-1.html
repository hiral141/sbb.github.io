Experiment Number : 1

Title of the Experiment : DISSOLVED OXYGEN

Practical Significance:

Dissolved oxygen analysis measures the amount of gaseous oxygen dissolved in an water. Oxygen gets into water by diffusion from the surrounding air, by aeration, and as a waste product of photosynthesis. Organic waste requires oxygen during its degradation in water. DO value is very important parameter during determination of biological oxygen demand.

Relevant Program Outcomes (POs):

PO 1: Basic and Discipline specific knowledge: Apply knowledge of basic mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals and engineering specialization to solve the chemical engineering problems.

PO 4: Engineering Tools, Experimentation and Testing: Apply modern engineering tools and appropriate technique to conduct standard tests and measurements.

Practical Outcome

Determine the dissolved oxygen in given water sample.

Theory

The test is based on the addition of divalent manganese solutions followed by strong alkali to the water sample in a glass stoppered bottle. Dissolved oxygen present in the sample rapidly oxidizes an equivalent amount of dispersed divalent manganese hydroxide. Precipitate to hydroxide of higher valency sate in the presence of iodine ions & up to acidification the oxidized manganese reverts to the divalent state with the liberation of iodine equivalent to the original dissolved oxygen (D.O) content in the sample. The iodine is then titrated with a standard solution of thiosulphate using starch indicator.

Experimental Set up:



Reagents -

1. Manganese sulphate solution
2. Alkali iodide azide reagent
3. Standard sodium thiosulphate titrant (0.025N) indicator.

Apparatus -

 1. B.O.D bottles
2. Graduated pipettes
3. Burette and iodine flasks

Procedure -

  1. Collect the sample in 250-300 ml bottles.
  2. Add 2 ml manganese sulphate followed by 2 ml of alkali iodide azide reagent well above the surface of the liquid.
  3. Stopper with care to exclude air bubble and mix by inverting the bottles at least 15 times when the precipitate settles, carefully remove the stopper and immediately add 2 ml of conc. H2SO4.
  4. Stopper the bottle and mix gently by inversing until dissolution is complete.
  5. Take 200 ml of above solution; titrate with 0.025 N thiosulphate solution to the pale straw colour.
  6. Add 2-3 ml starch and continue the titration to the first disappearance of the blue colour.
  7. This method is also known as the Winkler's/ Iodometric titration method.


Reaction -

 MnSO4 + 2KOH → Mn(OH)2 + K2SO4
(white ppt.)

Mn(OH)2 + ½ O2 → MnO(OH)2 (Magnaite)

MnO(OH)2 + 2H2SO4 → Mn(SO4)2 + 3H2O

Mn(SO4)2 + 2KI → MnSO4 + K2SO4 + I2

2Na2S2O3 + I2 → Na2S4O3 + 2 NaI
(0.025N)

Calculation -

1 ml 0.025N Na2S2O3= 1 x 0.025 x 8/1000 x 1 g O2

B.R. ml of Na2S2O3= B.R. x 0.025 x 8/1000 g O2

200ml sample= B.R. x 0.025 x 8/1000 g O2
