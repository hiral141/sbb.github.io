Experiment Number : 6

Title of the Experiment : CHLORIDE CONTENT

Practical Significance:

Chloride is categorized as pollutant for many reasons. Chloride is necessary for water habitats to thrive, yet high levels of chloride can have negative effects on an ecosystem. Chloride may impact freshwater organisms and plants by altering reproduction rates, increasing species mortality, and changing the characteristics of the entire local ecosystem. Chloride determination in natural waters is useful in the selection of water supplies for human use. Chloride determination is used to select the types of desalting apparatus to used.

Practical Outcome

To estimate the chloride content of the given water sample.

Theory

It is useful in detecting the chlorine content by using a standard silver nitrate solution and by using potassium chromate indicator. First the free content of chloride ion in sample water and AgNO3 solution forms a white ppt. of AgCl when all the chloride is reacted the chromate ion forms a brick red ppt. and silver chromate with excess drops of AgNO3. This both chlorides and chromate ions cause ppt with AgNO3. As AgCl is less soluble salt and initially the concentration of chloride ions is high, AgCl is precipitated. After the complete ppt of chloride ions the K2Cr2O7 reacts with AgNO3 solution and ferrous brick and silver chromate ppt. This ppt. of AgCrO4 gets absorbed to AgCl due to which white ppt. of AgCl acquire reddish brown colour.


Reagents -


  1. AgNO3 solutions (0.04N)
  2. 5% potassium chromate as indicator
  3. Solid CaCO3


Apparatus -


  1. Burette
  2. Pipette
  3. Beaker
  4. Conical Flask


Procedure -


  1. Fill the burette with standard 0.04N AgNO3 solution.
  2. Take 10 ml of given sample in conical flask.
  3. Add pinch of CaCO3 powder in conical flask.
  4. Add 3 to 4 ml of potassium chromate solution (indicator)
  5. Titrate the solution against AgNO3 solution (0.04N)
  6. End point of titration is permanent reddish brown colour ppt.
  7. Repeat the procedure for 50 ml tap water.
