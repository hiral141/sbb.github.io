Experiment Number : 7

Title of the Experiment : CONCENTRATION OF SODIUM ION

Practical Significance:

The soda ash industry is a part of the chemical industry, which is responsible for the production of sodium carbonate, calcium chloride, absorbent masses, evaporated wet salt, food salt, pickling salt or salt tablets. During manufacturing of those products, strongly alkaline wastewater is generated. It could be characterised by a high electrolytic conductivity and concentration of ions: chlorides, sulfates, phosphates, calcium, sodium, magnesium, potassium and ammonium.

Relevant Program Outcomes (POs):

PO 1: Basic and Discipline specific knowledge: Apply knowledge of basic mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals and engineering specialization to solve the chemical engineering problems.

PO 4: Engineering Tools, Experimentation and Testing: Apply modern engineering tools and appropriate technique to conduct standard tests and measurements.

Practical Outcome

To determine the concentration of sodium in the sample.

Theory

Flame photometer is an instrument with which it is possible to estimate with speed and accurate, minute quantities of Na, K and Ca. The principal solution of under analysis is sprayed as a fine mist to a non- luminous flame, which becomes coloured according to the characteristic's emission of the metal. A very narrow band of wavelength corresponding to the element analysed by the light filter and allowed to fall on a photo detector whose output is connected to an electric measuring device to provide the read out, before analysing the unknown solution the system is standardized with solution of known concentration. The total system consists of the following units:-

  1. Burner unit
  2. Compressor unit
  3. Electronic metering unit

Procedure -


  1. Burner unit
    1. Connect the compressor unit to the burner and turn on the compressor
    2. Adjust the air pressure to 0.5 kg/cm3. A jet of air should come out of atomizer.Â 
    3. Dip the atomizer sleeve in distilled water. A fine spray should come out. Wait for sometime some water starts draining out through the outlet of the backside of the burner input.
    4. Connect the LPG. cylinder to the burner unit (gas outlet is kept fully closed).
    5. Turn gas regulator of burner fully clockwise (closed position).
    6. Open outlet of gas source.
    7. Introduce a lighten matchstick through inspection window of burner unit and slowly turn it's gas regulator to produce a flame with a large control blue flame
    8. Adjust air pressure (air control) so that 10 blue comes are formed.

  2. Metering unit - Once burner unit is ignited following steps are to be taken :-
    1. Connect the measuring unit to the photo detector
    2. Turn the set zero coarse, set zero fine, set F.S coarse, set F.S fine control in maximum clockwise position.
    3. Set sensitivity at high
    4. Select appropriate filter
    5. Feed distilled water to atomizer and adjust set zero fine to display zero.
    6. Feed standard solution to the atomizer
    7. Set sensitivity control at low.
    8. Turn set F.S coarse and set F.S fine to display the desired full-scale reading (always) adjust full scale with lowest possible sensitivity. If the reading is not less than the desired full- scale higher of sodium sensitivity medium, high or low.
    9. Prepare calibration curves in the range of zero to one, zero to 10 and zero to 100 mg liter by using various standard solution of Na.
    10. Feed sample solution to atomizer to get the relative concentration.


Precautions -

 Before feeding each sample distilled water to the atomizer. Sample should be free from suspended impurities, if the sample contains any suspended matter filter it. Express the result as mg Na per liter of sample.
