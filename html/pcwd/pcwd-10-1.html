Experiment Number : 10

Title of the Experiment : PHENOL CONTENT

Practical Significance:

Most of the vegetables and some fruits are preferably consumed after some kind of processing, which can cause favourable or disadvantageous changes in the flavour and texture, increasing the food's palatability and affecting the quantity and quality of bioactive compounds, such as phenolics. The biological, physical, and chemical modifications that occur during some processing methods, as the cooking, are predominantly related with sensorial, nutritional, and textural alterations, which may be beneficial or harmful to the human health.

Relevant Program Outcomes (POs):

PO 1: Basic and Discipline specific knowledge: Apply knowledge of basic mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals and engineering specialization to solve the chemical engineering problems.

PO 4: Engineering Tools, Experimentation and Testing: Apply modern engineering tools and appropriate technique to conduct standard tests and measurements.

Practical Outcome

To determine the amount of phenol in the effluent

Principle -

Phenol reacts with 4 amino antipyrine at a pH of 10 in the presence of potassium ferricyanide to form a red antipyrine dye. This dye is extracted to from aqueous solution with 25ml chloroform and the absorbance is measured at 466nm using colorimeter/ spectrophotometer.

Method -

4 amino antipyrine colorimetric method. This method is applicable to the most of the phenol compound excepting para cresol acid and similar para substituted phenols.


Interference -

All the interference are eliminated or reduced minimum level by preliminary treatment distillation.

Reagent -


  1. Phenol
  2. Ammonia - ammonium chloride buffer
  3. 4 amino antipyrine solution
  4. Potassium ferricyanide solution
  5. Chloroform
  6. Sodium sulphate anhydrous granular.


Preparation of solution -


  1. Phenol stock solution: Dissolve 1gm phenol in distilled water and make up 1000ml in volumetric flask.
    1ml stock solution = 1 mg/l phenol

  2. Phenol intermediate solution: Dilute 10ml stock phenol solution in distilled water and make up 1000ml in volumetric flask. (100times dilution)
    1ml stock solution= 10µg phenol.

  3. Phenol working solution: Dilute 50ml intermediate phenol solution to 500ml with freshly boiled and cooled distilled water. 1ml = 1µg phenol (10 times dilution)

  4. Ammonia- ammonium chloride buffer: Dissolve 16.9gm ammonium chloride (NH4Cl) in 143 ml conc. Ammonia and dilute to 250ml with distilled water.

  5. 4 amino- antipyrine solution: Dissolve 2gm of 4 amino pyrenes in distilled water and dilute to 100ml with distilled water, prepare daily.

  6. Potassium ferricyanide solution: Dissolve 8 mg potassium ferricyanide (K3F8(CN)6) in distilled water and dilute to 100ml.


Procedure -


  1. In 100ml volumetric flask, take the phenol working solution as per the table and make up to 100ml by distilled water.

  2. Take 100ml solution from first volumetric flask in the separating funnel and add 2 ml of ammonia- ammonium chloride buffer solution.

  3. Add 3ml of 4 amino antipyrine solution mix immediately.

  4. Add 3ml potassium ferricyanide and again mix immediately.

  5. Allow to stand for 5 min.

  6. Extract with 25ml chloroform. Collect the extract in test tube.

  7. Standardize the colorimeter. Set zero.

  8. Set 100% transmittance with blank solution.

  9. Measure the optical density for each solution from volumetric flask

  10. Measure the optical density for unknown sample.

  11. Prepare a calibration curve by plotting OD against Concentration.

  12. Find phenol concentration of unknown sample from calibration curve.
