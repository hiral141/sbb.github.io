Experiment Number : 2

Title of the Experiment : CHEMICAL OXYGEN DEMAND - 1

Practical Significance:

The COD value indicates the amount of oxygen which is needed for the oxidation of all organic substances in water in mg/l. The COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand) is closely related to the laboratory standard method named Dichromate method. With this method the chemical oxygen demand is determined during chromic acid digestion of organic loads in waste water. Based on this method the COD became a commonly used sum parameter in waste water analysis. It is used for planning of waste water treatment plants for controlling the cleaning efficiency and for the calculation of waste water taxes.

Relevant Program Outcomes (POs):

PO 1: Basic and Discipline specific knowledge: Apply knowledge of basic mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals and engineering specialization to solve the chemical engineering problems.

PO 4: Engineering Tools, Experimentation and Testing: Apply modern engineering tools and appropriate technique to conduct standard tests and measurements.

Practical Outcome

Determine the dissolved oxygen in given water sample.

Theory

A sample is refluxed with known amounts of potassium dichromate and sulfuric acid and the excess of dichromate is titrated with ferrous ammonium sulphate. The amount of oxidizable matter measured as an oxygen equivalent is proportional to the potassium dichromate consumed. The straight chain compounds are more effective in the presence of silver sulphate catalyst. The oxidation and other difficulties caused by the presence of chlorides in the sample is overcomes by the addition of mercuric sulphate to the sample before refluxing.

Experimental Set up:



Reagents -


  1. Std. K2Cr2O7 (0.025N)
  2. Standard ferrous ammonium sulphate titrate (0.1N)
  3. Ferrous indicator
  4. Conc. Sulfuric acid
  5. Std. Silver sulphate reagent


Apparatus -


  1. Reflux apparatus
  2. 250 ml of Erlenmeyer flask with condensers
  3. Heating mantle
  4. Pipette
  5. Burette, etc


Procedure -


  1. Place 0.4 gms of H2SO4 in the reflux flask.
  2. Pipette out 5 ml of the sample into the reflux flask.
  3. Pipette out 10 ml of std. K2Cr2O7, add several glass beads slowly and add 30ml of sulphuric acid - silver sulphide reagent in the cylinder through the sides of the conical flask and mix it thoroughly during the addition.
  4. Add 20 to 30 ml of water slowly through the side mix of the reflux mixture thoroughly before heat is applied.
  5. If this is not done, local heating occurs in the bottom of the flask and the mixture will be blown out of the condenser.
  6. Connect the flask to the condenser and reflux the mixture for 2hrs Cool and wash down the condenser with distilled water.
  7. Make the final volume to 150 ml approx in the same conical flask before titration.
  8. Titrate the excess dichromate with std. Ferrous ammonium sulphate using 2-3 drops of ferroin indicator.
  9. Take as the end point, the sharp colour change from blue green to reddish brown.
  10. Even through the blue green may reappear within minutes.
  11. Reflux in the same manner a blank containing distilled water in place of the sample together with reagents.


Reaction -

 K2Cr2O7 + 4H2SO4 → K2SO4 + Cr2(SO4)3 + 4H2O + 3 (O)

6Fe(NH4)(SO4)2 + 3H2SO4 + 3(O) → 3Fe2(SO4)3 + 6(NH4)2SO4 + 3H2O

K2Cr2O7 + 6Fe(NH4)2(SO4)2 + 7H2SO4 → K2SO4 + Cr2(SO4)3 + 6(NH4)2SO4 + 7H2O
