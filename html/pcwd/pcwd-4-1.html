Title of the Experiment : BIOLOGICAL OXYGEN DEMAND-1

Practical Significance:

BOD is the only test that gives a measure of amount of biologically oxidizable organic matter present in wastewater that can be used to determine the rate at which oxidation will occur or BOD will be exerted in receiving bodies of water. It is the principal test applied to domestic and industrial waste to determine strength in terms of oxygen required for stabilization. BOD is the major criterion used in stream pollution control where organic loading must be restricted to maintain the desired DO levels.

Practical Outcome

Determine the biological oxygen demand in given water sample.

Theory

The biological oxygen demand (BOD) of sewage or of polluted water is the amount of oxygen required for the biological decomposition of dissolved organic matter to occur under aerobic condition and at the standardized time and temperature. Usually the time us taken is taken as 5 days and the temperature 20 degree Celsius as per the global standard. The BOD test is among the most important method in sanitary analysis to determine the polluting power, or strength of sewage, industrial waste or polluted water. It serves as a measure of the amount of clean diluting water required for the successful disposal of sewage by dilution. The test has its widest application in measuring waste loading to treatment plants and in evaluating the efficiency of such treatment systems. The test consists in taking the given sample in suitable concentration in dilute water in BOD bottles. Two bottles are taken for each concentration and three concentration are used for each sample l. One set of bottle is incubated in a BOD incubator for 5 days at 20 degree Celsius; the dissolved oxygen (initial) content (D1) in the other set of bottles will be determined immediately. At the end of 5 days, the dissolved oxygen content (D2) in the incubated bottle is determined.Then, BOD = D1 - D2/P

Where,
P = Decimal fraction of sample used
D1 = Dissolved oxygen of diluted sample (mg/l), immediately after preparation
D2 = Dissolved oxygen of diluted sample (mg/l), at the end of 5 days incubation

Among the three values of BOD obtained for a sample select that dilution showing the residue dissolved oxygen of at least 1 mg/l and a depletion of at least 2 mg/l. If two or more dilutions are showing the same conditions then select the BOD value obtained by that dilutions in which the maximum dissolved oxygen depletion obtained. Waste water from sewage treatment plants often contains organic materials that are decomposed by microorganisms, which use oxygen in the process (the amount of oxygen consumed by these organisms is breaking down the waste is known as the Biological Oxygen Demand or BOD).

Dilution Water -

As most of effluent contain large quantities of organic matter and do not contain dissolved oxygen, they have to be diluted and supplied with oxygen. This is done by diluting them with standard dilution water containing enough oxygen. Suitable dilution water should satisfy the following requirements:

  1. Correct pH limits
  2. Proper salinity
  3. Reasonable buffer capacity
  4. The presence of mineral nutrients
  5. Absence of toxic metals and free chlorine

Nutrient materials: Mineral nutrients are necessary for the growth and activities of bacteria.

Seeding with Micro-organisms: It is essential to seed the dilution water when BOD determination is made on samples of industrial effluents, which do not contain bacteria. The seeding material generally used is fresh and settled raw sewage.


Procedure -


  1. Take 5 lit of distilled water.
  2. Aerate for 3 hrs.
  3. Add 1 ml each of calcium chloride, Magnesium sulphate, ferric chloride and phosphate buffer solution per litre of aerated distilled water. Prepare this solution water just before use.
  4. Add 2ml of seeding water per litre dilution water.
  5. If the pH of the sample is not in the range of 6.5 to 8.5, add sufficient amount of H2SO4 or NaOH to bring the pH in that range.
  6. Take two sets of samples in BOD bottle. One set used for the immediate DO and the other kept for incubation at 27deg C for 3 days or 20 deg C for 5 days.
  7. Glucose - glutamic acid check: Take diluted water in a litre standard flask.
  8. Add 20ml ( since 2% sample) of glucose-glutamic acid per litre of the solution water.
  9. Adjust the pH.
  10. Add dilution water to make 1litre exactly without any air bubble.
  11. Take two sets of samples in BOD bottle. One set used for the immediate DO and the other kept for incubation at 27deg C for 3 days or 20 deg C for 5 days.
  12. Instead of glucose-glutamic acid take the effluent, and repeat the procedure.
